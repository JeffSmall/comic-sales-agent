## 1. Product vision

**Comic Sales Agent** is an **iOS app for tracking the market value of collectible comic books** —
both professionally graded (CGC / CBCS) and raw (ungraded) copies. It helps a collector watch a
personal list of key issues and understand how each one is selling right now, broken down by the
dimension that actually drives comic prices: **grade**.

Its defining characteristic: **the experience is generated by an AI agent, and rendered as real
native UI — not chat text.** The user interacts in two complementary ways:

- **Tap** the interface to navigate and act (tap a comic to see its detail, tap "back" to return).
- **Type** a natural-language request when tapping isn't enough (add a comic, ask about a trend).

The agent decides *what to show* and *what data to bind*; a fixed catalog of high-density,
data-first widgets decides *how it looks*. The result is consistent, native, on-brand UI for a
data-dense, finance-flavored domain.

**One-line positioning:** *A tap-driven, AI-generated dashboard for your comic watchlist — see what
your keys are worth, by grade, at a glance.*

---

## 2. Who it's for

- **Primary (and only, in v1) user:** a single comic collector/investor (the project owner) who
  tracks a watchlist of "key" issues (first appearances, significant covers) and wants to monitor
  their market value over time.
- **Mindset:** finance-flavored. They think in grades, price ranges, recent comps, and trends —
  "what did a 9.8 New Mutants #98 last sell for, and are 9.8s softening vs 9.4s?"
- **Context of use:** quick, glanceable check-ins on a phone; occasional deeper dives into a single
  book's price history; occasional adds/edits as the collection changes.

> v2+ may add multiple users and auth, but **design for a single-user, personal-tool feel** now.

---

## 3. Goals & non-goals

### Goals (what the design must serve)
- Make the **watchlist the home** — open the app, immediately see your tracked comics and their
  latest value. No empty screen, no required typing.
- Let the user **drill into any comic** to understand its current market: a price summary and a
  **grade-by-grade** breakdown of recent sales.
- Support **conversational add / edit / remove** of comics with zero forms required (typing), while
  leaving room for a more guided add experience.
- Present **dense, scannable, Tufte-style data** (sparklines, grade-tier matrices, right-aligned
  numerics) rather than prose.
- Provide a graceful **first-run / empty experience** that onboards a brand-new user.

### Non-goals (out of scope for the design right now)
- Android, web, desktop, Apple Watch (iOS-only by design).
- Multi-user, login/auth, account management.
- Buying/selling, transactions, or marketplace integration (this is a *tracker*, not a store).
- Push notifications / price alerts UI — **designed for conceptually (v2)**, but not a v1 screen.

## 4. Core concepts (domain model)

The design should speak this vocabulary. These are the real entities and the data available to show.

| Concept | What it is | Key attributes |
|---|---|---|
| **Comic / Book** | A specific issue the user tracks | Title (e.g. "New Mutants"), Issue (e.g. "#98"), Publisher (e.g. "Marvel"), Notes (e.g. "1st app. Deadpool") |
| **Owned copy** | The specific copy the user owns/tracks | Graded or Raw; Grader (CGC/CBCS) if graded; Grade (0.5–10.0, e.g. 9.4) |
| **Grade** | Condition score that **dominates price** | Numeric, typically 0.5–10.0 in 0.2 steps; a 9.8 can be 5–10× the price of a 9.4 |
| **Graded vs Raw** | Whether a copy is professionally slabbed | "graded" (CGC/CBCS, has a numeric grade) vs "raw" (ungraded) |
| **Edition** | Print variant that affects price | newsstand / direct / unknown (newsstand often commands a premium) |
| **Sale** | One completed market sale (a "comp") | Price, Date, Source (eBay/manual), URL, the **grade of that sale**, edition |
| **Price history** | The recent sales for a book | ~90-day window of real eBay sold listings; refreshed over time |
| **Watchlist** | The user's whole tracked set | A list of Comics; the app's home |

**The load-bearing idea:** price is analyzed **per grade**. Every sale carries its own grade, so the
product can show "9.8 median vs 9.4 median," grade-tier matrices, and "higher grades softening"
trends. Designs should treat **grade as a first-class axis**, not an afterthought.

**Derived values to display:** last sale price, median, min–max range, and % change over the window —
all computed from the sales history (per book, and per grade).

## 5. Design principles

1. **UI is input, not just output.** Rendered elements are affordances — tappable to navigate and
   act. Typing is the fallback, not the default path. (Decision D2.)
2. **The watchlist is the home screen.** Launch straight into it. (Decision D6.)
3. **Drill-in navigation with a clear way back.** List → detail → back. No deep stacks, no
   dead-ends. The "back" control is part of the generated UI. (Decision D1.)
4. **Tufte data-ink doctrine** (the visual north star):
   - Maximize data-ink; remove chartjunk (no gridlines, borders, shadows, or decoration).
   - **Monochrome with a single accent color**, reserved for anomalies / selection / price moves.
   - **Direct labeling** — labels sit next to the data, never in a distant legend.
   - **Right-align numerics**; align decimal points for scannability.
   - **High density** — sparklines, small multiples, dense matrices; show macro and micro at once.
5. **Native and consistent.** Material 3 widgets from a **fixed catalog**; the experience never
   renders raw HTML or ad-hoc one-off styling. Visual changes happen by extending the catalog.
6. **Glanceable first, deep on demand.** The home view answers "what's it worth?" in a glance; the
   detail view answers "why, and by grade."

## 6. The widget catalog (the design vocabulary)

The product is composed from a small, deliberate set of widgets. **Design new screens primarily
from these building blocks** (and propose additions when needed — additions extend the catalog).

| Widget | Role | Visual intent |
|---|---|---|
| **WatchlistRow** | One comic in the list | Title/issue left, latest price right, inline sparkline; tappable; no gridlines |
| **Sparkline** | Word-sized trend line | A tiny inline trend; single accent dot for the latest/anomalous point |
| **MetricCard** | One number + label (+ optional delta) | E.g. "Median $1,000 ▲ +12%"; no borders/shadows |
| **GradeTierMatrix** | Grade × recent-sales grid | The centerpiece for grade analysis; GitHub-contribution-style density |
| **SmallMultiplesGrid** | Repeated mini-charts | Compare books or grades at a glance (macro + micro) |
| **TextBlock** | Fallback prose | Used sparingly — welcome copy, explanations; never the default |

> **Status:** Today the app renders with **basic primitives only** (Card / Row / Column / Text /
> Button) — the custom data-ink widgets above (Sparkline, GradeTierMatrix, SmallMultiplesGrid) are
> **Target/Planned**. Wireframe the target widgets; we'll build them into the catalog as we go.

## 7. Information architecture & navigation

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  App bar:  "Comic Sales Agent"                           │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                          │
│   HOME = WATCHLIST            ⇆ (tap a comic)            │
│        │                                                 │
│        └── BOOK DETAIL  ── (tap "← Watchlist") ──┐       │
│                                                  │       │
│   (empty watchlist → WELCOME / FIRST-RUN view)   │       │
│                                                  ▼       │
│                                            back to HOME  │
│                                                          │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Conversational input bar (always present)  [ send ▸ ]  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

- **Single primary surface** that swaps content in place as you navigate (home ⇄ detail). It reads
  as **drill-in**, not a growing scroll of stacked answers.
- **The input bar is always available** at the bottom for natural-language requests (add/edit, ask
  a trend question) regardless of which view you're on.
- **Back** is an in-content affordance ("← Watchlist") on the detail view, not a system nav bar.

## 8. Screens & states

For each: purpose, content, components, interactions, and states. **Built** vs **Target** is marked.

### 8.1 Home — Watchlist (populated)  · *Built (lean), Target (rich)*
- **Purpose:** the default landing view; see every tracked comic and its latest value at a glance.
- **Content per row:** title + issue, owned grade (e.g. "CGC 9.4" or "Raw"), and **last sale price**
  (right-aligned). *Target:* add an inline **Sparkline** and a price-move accent (▲/▼ color).
- **Components:** a vertical list of **WatchlistRow**s (each tappable).
- **Interactions:** tap a row → Book Detail. Type in the input bar → add/edit/ask.
- **Sort/scan:** alphabetical today; *Target:* consider sort options (biggest movers, value, recently
  updated) as tappable chips.
- **States:** loading (spinner while the watchlist loads on launch); error (a single card explaining
  the watchlist couldn't load).

### 8.2 First-run — Welcome / Empty state  · *Built (copy), Target (polished onboarding)*
- **Purpose:** the only legitimately "empty" screen — a brand-new user with zero comics.
- **Content:** a warm one-line welcome; a line stating "you're not tracking any comics yet"; clear
  instructions to **add your first comic** by entering its details — **Comic Title, Issue Number,
  Grade, and Graded or Raw** — with a concrete example (*"e.g. Add Amazing Fantasy #15, CGC 9.0
  (graded)"*).
- **Interactions:** the user types the comic into the input bar; the agent adds it and the view
  becomes the populated watchlist.
- **Target enhancement:** consider a light, guided **"Add your first comic"** affordance (a tappable
  prompt or a simple field set) in addition to free typing.

### 8.3 Book Detail  · *Built (lean), Target (rich, grade-forward)*
- **Purpose:** understand a single comic's current market, **by grade**.
- **Content, top → bottom:**
  1. **Back affordance** — "← Watchlist".
  2. **Header** — title + issue (and publisher/notes as secondary).
  3. **Price summary** — Last sale, Median, Range (min–max), and % change over the window. *Target:*
     render as **MetricCard**s with a price-move accent and a window **Sparkline**.
  4. **"Median Graded Sales"** — a per-grade breakdown: one line per grade showing that grade's
     median and range (e.g. "9.6  $2,150  (range $936–$2,495)"). *Target:* a **GradeTierMatrix**
     visualizing grade × recent sales, with the accent color for anomalies.
  5. *Target:* **raw** vs **graded** comparison, edition (newsstand/direct) notes, and 30/60/90-day
     window toggles.
- **Interactions:** tap "← Watchlist" → Home. Type a refinement ("how are the 9.8s trending?") →
  agent re-renders with that focus.
- **States:** loading; "no recent sales for this book" (graceful empty); error.

### 8.4 Add / Edit a comic  · *Built (conversational), Target (optional guided form)*
- **Purpose:** add a new comic, or edit an existing one's owned-copy details.
- **Built:** fully conversational — type "Add Amazing Spider-Man #129, CGC 7.0" or "change New
  Mutants #98 to raw"; the agent confirms and the watchlist re-renders.
- **Target (design opportunity):** a light guided capture for the fields (Title, Issue, Publisher,
  Graded/Raw, Grader, Grade, Notes) for users who prefer structure — without abandoning the
  conversational path. Worth wireframing both.

### 8.5 Update Sales (refresh market data)  · *Target/Planned*
- **Purpose:** pull the latest ~90 days of real sales for the watchlist.
- **Design:** an **"Update Sales"** action (button or chip) that kicks off a background refresh and
  returns immediately, with a non-blocking progress/last-updated indicator ("Updating… ~M min" /
  "Sales updated 2h ago"). It runs in the background; the user keeps using the app.

### 8.6 Trend / price-history exploration  · *Target/Planned (the data viz centerpiece)*
- **Purpose:** answer "how is this selling, and by grade, over time?"
- **Design:** 30/60/90-day views; **grade-variance** visualization; **anomaly highlighting**
  (the single accent); the **GradeTierMatrix** and **SmallMultiplesGrid** in full. This is the
  richest design surface and the main reason the catalog exists — give it the most exploration.

### 8.7 System states (apply across screens)
- **Loading:** brief spinner during the launch fetch and any request round-trip.
- **Empty (per context):** first-run welcome (8.2); "no sales yet" on a detail.
- **Error:** a single, plain-language card ("Couldn't complete that — try again"), never a blank
  screen.

### 8.8 Price alerts / notifications  · *v2 — designed conceptually, not a v1 screen*
- Future: a push ("9.8 New Mutants #98 sold +18% above trend") that, when tapped, deep-links into
  that book's detail. Optional to wireframe now as a forward-looking concept.

## 9. Key user flows

**Flow A — First launch (new user, empty watchlist)**
1. Open app → brief loading → **Welcome/Empty** view.
2. Read the welcome + instructions; type the first comic into the input bar.
3. Agent adds it → view becomes the **populated Watchlist** with the new comic.

**Flow B — Daily check-in (returning user)**
1. Open app → **Watchlist** loads automatically (no prompt).
2. Scan latest prices / movers at a glance.
3. (optional) Tap a comic → **Book Detail** → review price summary + per-grade sales → tap
   "← Watchlist" to return.

**Flow C — Drill into a comic's grade-level market**
1. From the Watchlist, tap a comic.
2. **Book Detail** renders: summary + "Median Graded Sales" per grade.
3. (Target) Toggle 30/60/90 days, or ask "how are the 9.8s trending?" → re-renders focused.
4. Tap "← Watchlist".

**Flow D — Add / edit / remove a comic**
1. Type the intent ("Add …", "change … to raw", "remove …").
2. Agent confirms and mutates; the watchlist re-renders and persists.

**Flow E — Refresh market data (Target)**
1. Tap **Update Sales**.
2. A non-blocking indicator shows it's running; the user keeps browsing.
3. When done, prices/trends reflect the newest sales.

**Flow F — Price alert (v2, conceptual)**
1. Receive a push about a notable sale/trend.
2. Tap it → app opens directly to that book's **Detail**.

## 11. Visual design direction (for the designer)

- **Aesthetic:** clean, dense, editorial/financial — think a well-set data table or a Tufte plate,
  not a flashy consumer app. Generous data, minimal chrome.
- **Color:** near-monochrome base + **one accent color** reserved for meaning (price up/down,
  selection, anomalies). Today's app uses a warm off-white background with a deep-orange seed; the
  accent and full palette are open for design.
- **Typography:** a clear hierarchy — strong title/issue, quieter metadata, tabular/lining figures
  for prices (decimal-aligned). Numbers are the hero.
- **Price movement:** encode up/down with the accent (e.g., green-up/red-down is acceptable as the
  one semantic color pair) plus direction glyphs and deltas.
- **Grade as a visual language:** consider how grade tiers read at a glance (a 9.8 vs a 6.0) — the
  GradeTierMatrix is where this matters most.
- **Modes:** design **light and dark**.
- **Identity:** app icon and launch screen are open (currently default).
- **Motion:** subtle — drill-in transitions, the list settling to the top on navigation.

## 12. Constraints a designer should respect

- **iOS-only, phone-first.** Single-hand, portrait, glanceable.
- **Catalog-constrained rendering.** Every screen is composed from the shared widget catalog; the
  agent binds data into it. "New look" = a new/edited catalog widget, not a one-off. Wireframes
  should therefore describe **reusable components**, not bespoke per-screen art.
- **Conversational + tap dual input.** Don't design away the input bar; design *around* it. Many
  actions are best as taps, but natural language is always available and sometimes the best path.
- **Data is real and grade-keyed.** ~90-day eBay sold history; not every book has sales at every
  grade — design for sparse/empty grades gracefully.
- **Current technical limit (improving):** the present rendering path caps a single view's payload
  size, so the *shipped* watchlist/detail are intentionally lean (single text lines). This is a
  known limitation we plan to lift; **design for the rich target** — just know the first
  implementation may approximate it while the constraint is removed.

## 14. Open design questions (resolve during wireframing)

1. **Transcript vs dashboard feel.** We chose drill-in (single surface that replaces). Should the
   conversational answers ever persist as scrollable history, or always replace? (Leaning: replace.)
2. **Guided add vs pure conversation.** Is a structured add-comic form worth it, or does
   conversational + good empty-state copy suffice?
3. **Where do "Update Sales," sort, and filters live** — a top toolbar, inline chips, an overflow
   menu? How prominent?
4. **How to visualize grade tiers** at watchlist glance (a tiny sparkline per row?) vs detail
   (full GradeTierMatrix).
5. **Empty/sparse grades.** How to show a book/grade with few or no recent comps without looking
   broken.
6. **Accent semantics.** One accent for everything (selection + anomaly + price move), or a small
   semantic set (up/down)?

## 15. Glossary

- **Grade** — a comic's condition score (0.5–10.0); the dominant price driver.
- **Graded / Raw** — professionally slabbed (CGC/CBCS, numeric grade) vs ungraded.
- **Grader** — the grading company (CGC or CBCS).
- **Edition** — newsstand vs direct print variant (affects price).
- **Comp / Sale** — a completed market sale used as a comparable.
- **Key (issue)** — a notable/valuable issue (first appearance, key cover, etc.).
- **Watchlist** — the user's set of tracked comics; the app's home.
- **Generative UI** — the agent emits a declarative UI description from a fixed widget catalog; the
  app renders it natively. The agent controls structure + data, never pixels.
